Welcome to Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan, officially the Republic of Kazakhstan, is a landlocked country primarily in Central Asia, with a small portion situated in Eastern Europe. It borders Russia to the north and west, China to the east, Kyrgyzstan to the southeast, Uzbekistan to the south, and Turkmenistan to the southwest, with a coastline along the Caspian Sea. Its capital is Astana, while the largest city and leading cultural and commercial hub is Almaty.

Beautiful places of Kazakhstan

  1. Astana

    Astana, formerly known as Nur-Sultan, Akmolinsk, Tselinograd, and Akmola, is the capital and second-largest city of Kazakhstan with a population of 1,350,228 within the city limits after Almaty, which had been the capital until 1997. The city lies on the banks of the Ishim River in the north-central part of Kazakhstan, within the Akmola Region, though administered as a city with special status separately from the rest of the region. Initially founded as Akmoly in 1830, the city was later renamed Akmolinsk, Tselinograd, and Akmola before adopting the name Astana in 1998, which means "capital city" in Kazakh.

  2. BAO

    The Great Almaty Lake, or BAO for short, is one of the most picturesque natural wonders of Kazakhstan, located in the mountains of the Trans-Ili Alatau, 15 kilometers south of the city of Almaty. The lake is known for its crystal clear turquoise waters, which change color depending on the season and lighting, attracting travelers, photographers and nature lovers. The lake is located at an altitude of 2,511 meters above sea level. It is about 1.6 kilometers long, 1 kilometer wide, and 30 meters deep. The water in the lake is always cold, even in summer the temperature rarely rises above 10 °C, which preserves its transparency and unique color.

  3. Kolsay Lake

    Often referred to as the "Pearls of Tien Shan," this park features a series of three alpine lakes—Lower, Middle, and Upper Kolsay—set amidst lush coniferous forests and mountain meadows. The area is ideal for hiking, camping, and horse riding

  4. Charyn Canyon

    Located about 200 km east of Almaty, Charyn Canyon stretches approximately 154 km along the Charyn River. Its dramatic rock formations, particularly in the "Valley of Castles," offer breathtaking views and are a popular spot for hiking and photography. .

  5. Altyn Emel National Park

    This vast park covers approximately 4,600 square kilometers and is home to diverse landscapes, including the famous Singing Sand Dune, which produces a humming sound when the wind blows. The park also features colorful mountains and a variety of wildlife.
